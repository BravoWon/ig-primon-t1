# Valor Energy — Prospect Intelligence Dossier (Public Domain, Maximized)

*Companion to `VALOR_PROSPECTS_ANALYSIS.md` (the KGS data screen). This dossier maps the full public
knowledge per prospect as a known-knowns / known-unknowns / unknown-unknowns matrix, adds the
commodity-economics reality, and gives the exact public endpoints to close each gap. Compiled
2026-06-22 from four cited web-research sweeps + local KGS data. Every external number is
source-attributed in the research logs; pull-paths are live URLs.*

## Executive reframe — what the intelligence changes
1. **The 7.5% ORRI is the deal's central economic problem.** At WTI ~$74 / Kansas Common ~$66.75
   (−$8–11 differential), a conventional vertical (D&C ~$400–550K, LOE ~$3K/mo) breaks even around
   **$50–55 Kansas Common** and is underwater below ~$45. A **7.5% override** drops NRI to
   **0.74–0.80** and lifts breakeven **~9–10%** — enough to "turn a marginally economic stripper into
   a non-starter." **Implication: these prospects must be *good wells*, not stripper-grade — and the
   ORRI is the cleanest lever to renegotiate** (7.5% is high; 1–3% is typical).
2. **Finney/Gray Morrow is OIL-prone** (north of the regional gas line) — the oil-only data gap is
   milder than first flagged, and **"Algrim" is a real named Finney field** (pullable). Upside is
   real *if on a channel* (analog Stewart Field: seismic-defined Morrow channel, **>7 MMBO**, now a
   CO2-EOR unit). Hinges on seismic, exactly as screened.
3. **Butler T28S R5–6E is OFF the El Dorado crest** — southern Nemaha "granite ridge" near Douglass,
   stripper country (~98% water cut on old wells). Independent research verdict: *"structure-real ≠
   structure-pays"* — our own thesis, confirmed by a third party.
4. **Ohio Harpers Station is effectively a wildcat** — Ross Co: **102 wells, 0 active, 0 production**,
   ~100 mi from the Trenton trend; Trenton is deep and untested here. Justified only as a cheap,
   ORRI-carried option, not a development target.

## Prospect 1–2 — Old Paint / Algrim (Morrow, Finney+Gray) — **PRIORITY**
**Known knowns (public):**
- Play = Lower-Penn incised-valley/channel sandstone; productivity controlled by **channel-fairway
  position** (valley axis = pay; off-axis = dry). Channels ¼–½ mi wide (Stewart) to <1,000 ft (Eubank).
- **Finney/Gray are oil-prone**; Morrow gas dominates the southern tier (Clark/Morton). Oil ~28–42° API.
- **Analog type curve:** single Morrow oil well IP ~**50–250 BOPD**, EUR **~50–150 MBO** (40-ac, 15%
  primary RF), waterflood/CO2-EOR upside. Field-scale (good channel): up to **~7 MMBO** (Stewart).
- USGS Anadarko Morrowan–Atokan AU (province-wide, undiscovered mean): **15 MMBO / 332 BCF / 7 MMB NGL**.
- Activity: Finney ~1,518 producing wells (Merit, Berexco, Scout, American Warrior); Gray sparse (~54).
  Stewart "Mackey/Stewart Morrow Unit" is an **active CO2-EOR / carbon-capture project** today.
- Our screen: footprint median best12 **4,153 bbl** (oil floor); **Gray = open running room**.

**Known unknowns (+ where to close):**
- *Channel-fairway position vs the acquired sections* — the whole game. **Seismic only** (Stewart/Damme/
  Eubank were all 3D amplitude-defined). Public data cannot map sand at lease resolution.
- *Algrim field actual production* — pull live: KGS Field query `chasm.kgs.ku.edu/ords/oil.ogf4.IDProdQuery?FieldNumber=<Algrim id>`.
- *Gas volumes* — KGS Magellan lease query (oil **and** gas MCF) `chasm.kgs.ku.edu/ords/oil.ogl5.SelectLeases` (Finney=55, Gray=69, T23–24S R29–30W).
- *Net pay / φ / Sw / pressure at the sections* — KGS LAS/raster logs + DST DB (`apps.kgs.ku.edu/web/DST/`); note the documented Morrow **underpressure pitfall** (good zones logged as "wet").

**Verdict:** the one prospect whose well quality can plausibly carry the 7.5% ORRI. Pull Algrim + gas +
logs; the seller's seismic channel maps are the gating asset.

## Prospect 3 — Butler County Arbuckle — **GATE HARD on seismic**
**Known knowns (public):**
- Reservoir = "Stapleton zone," karsted/weathered Arbuckle on the **sub-Pennsylvanian unconformity**,
  porosity preserved on **basement structural highs** (Nemaha ridge). Shallow (~2,300–2,500 ft on domes).
- **T28S R5–6E is on the southern trend near Douglass — OFF the El Dorado super-giant crest.** Treat
  El Dorado (~290–300 MMbbl cum, disc. 1915, now ~2,200 bo/d) as a *trend analog, not an offset*.
- Mature/stripper: Butler ~1,003 producing wells, **~700 Mbbl/yr flat**, ~**98% water cut** on old
  Arbuckle wells; modern infill EUR **~50 Mbbl/well**; operators Vess, Hartman, Darrah.
- **No public evidence of any 2022–2026 new-drill Arbuckle program** — county is recompletion/waterflood/stripper.
- Arbuckle CO2-EOR is **immiscible** (low pressure) and **no CO2 supply** reaches Butler — EOR case is greenfield.
- Our screen: footprint structurally **LOW (26th pct)**, mature (140 wells); structure→best12 +0.30 (n=31, underpowered).

**Known unknowns (+ where to close):**
- *Is the footprint on an untapped closure or a flank/saddle?* — **seismic + DST/Tops** only. The whole value driver.
- *Bypassed-pay / remaining So on-lease* — modern cased-hole logs/core (not public).
- *Waterflood status / voidage on offsets* — KGS Fluid-Injection (UIC) DB + CO-7 production for T28S R5–6E.
- *New permits?* — live **KCC Notices-of-Intents** search (`kcc.ks.gov/notices-of-intents-search`), Butler.

**Verdict:** mature, high-water, off-crest. With a 7.5% ORRI on stripper economics, this only works if
the seller's seismic identifies a specific **untapped/under-drained Arbuckle culmination**. Otherwise it
is the weakest prospect. Confirm before spending lease or seismic capital.

## Prospect 4 — Harpers Station (Trenton, Ross Co, Ohio) — **WILDCAT OPTION**
**Known knowns (public):**
- Ross Co = **frontier/non-productive**: 102 wells on file, **0 active, 0 operators, 0 production**;
  only a trivial 1994 gas blip (~123 MMcf, not Trenton), ~zero since 2010. "Tar Hollow State" /
  "Harpers Station" are prospect/lease names, **no field**.
- ~100+ mi from the Lima-Indiana Trenton trend (NW Ohio); on the **eastern flank of the Cincinnati
  Arch**, west of the Utica fairway. Trenton/Black River here is **deep and untested**; productive
  only where **basement-fault-controlled hydrothermal dolomite** exists (NY/Ontario/WV sweet spots:
  Glodes Corners disc. **3.29 MMcf/d**, EUR **4–10+ Bcf** — *sweet-spot numbers that do not transfer*).
- Deal note: this prospect is **ORRI-only** in the agreement (override boundary in Exhibit A), KS-style
  7.5% replaced by OH 5.5% — consistent with an option, not a committed development.

**Known unknowns (+ where to close):**
- *Has any Ross Co well even penetrated the Trenton?* (almost certainly not) — ODNR **RBDMS
  Completions** (`apps.ohiodnr.gov/oilgas/rbdmsreports/Reports_Completions.aspx`, County=Ross, all
  statuses) and **GIS layer-2** plugged-wells query (`gis2.ohiodnr.gov/.../Oilgas_Wells.../MapServer/2/query?where=COUNTY='ROSS'`).
- *Depth to Trenton / any fault-HTD potential under Concord-Buckskin Twps* — seismic/aeromag/gravity only; unverified.
- *The 16 permits on file* — operators/targets via ODNR.

**Verdict:** rank-wildcat. Value = cheap optionality on a deep, unproven HTD concept. Do **not** spend
real capital without ODNR deep-control review + seismic. Run the free ODNR pulls first to confirm
"never been tested," which is the likely answer.
