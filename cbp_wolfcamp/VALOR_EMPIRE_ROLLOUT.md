## Answering the Giza question, honestly
Your instinct — *productive rock registers differently than barren rock* — is exactly right and is the
whole basis of geophysics. But the research settles what's real:
- **It registers across many physics at once:** density (gravity), magnetic susceptibility
  (magnetics), acoustic impedance & gas (seismic), resistivity (EM), surface geochemistry (seepage),
  surface strain (InSAR). Productive rock is loud — *if you listen on enough channels.*
- **No single sensor "X-rays" it from orbit.** SAR penetrates **centimetres to a few metres** of *dry*
  material — the Giza "shafts 640 m–2 km down" claim (Biondi/Malanga) is off by **2–4 orders of
  magnitude**, was a *press release, not peer review*, and is rejected by the relevant experts. The
  *real* Giza imaging was **muon tomography** — peer-reviewed in *Nature* (2017 "Big Void"), confirmed
  by **three independent teams** with three technologies. That contrast *is the lesson.*
- **The real "synthetic aperture" of the subsurface is data fusion + drilling + verification.** You
  don't need one impossible aperture; you fuse many real ones and require each prediction to survive
  an out-of-sample test. **Muon-tomography discipline (multi-modal, independently verified, physics-
  grounded) is the model; SAR-Giza hype (sensational, unverified, physically impossible) is the
  anti-pattern the VERIFY gate exists to kill.**

## The full sensing stack — what each dimension sees, and its honest limit
| Dimension | What productive rock registers | Public data | Honest resolution / limit |
|---|---|---|---|

| **Seismic** | impedance (structure/stratigraphy); **gas directly (bright-spot/AVO)**; **channel sands** (Stewart Field was seismically defined) | SEG open (Teapot Dome); USGS NAMSS (marine) | **The km-scale workhorse.** Reservoir-scale. Your Morrow + Arbuckle value lives here — and it's the seller's proprietary asset |
| **EM / MT / CSEM** | resistivity: hydrocarbons resistive, brine conductive | academic/USGS | Structural/regional; CSEM "not unambiguous" (salt/carbonate also resistive) — the boom-bust cautionary tale |
| **Spectral / hyperspectral** | microseepage halos: bleached soils, clay/carbonate, vegetation stress; direct C–H absorption | ASTER, Sentinel-2, EMIT, EnMAP (free) | **Terrain-gated**: works arid/bare-soil, *fails under vegetation*; ~11–18% false neg/pos; mostly post-diction over known fields |

| **Well logs / production** | the ground truth: φ, pay, fluids, decline | **KGS (free LAS+tops+production), ODNR, RRC** | The anchor everything else calibrates to. We already have KGS |
