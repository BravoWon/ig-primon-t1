## Module E — Dedekind/CM generalization (registered v0.4; all items [GATE])

System: arithmetic gas with partition function ζ_K(β) for a number field K (simple pole at 1, residue by the class number formula); BC-type refinements exist in the literature (Connes–Marcolli–Ramachandran for imaginary quadratic; Laca–Larsen–Neshveyev for general K — verify attributions at registration of any claim).

**HE.1 [GATE: derive].** The constant term of the Fisher expansion generalizes through the Laurent data of ζ_K: conjectured c₀^K = −(γ_K² + 2γ_{K,1}) with γ_K Ihara's Euler–Kronecker constant and γ_{K,1} the Dedekind–Stieltjes analog. Derive precisely before any statement.

**HE.2 [REVISED v0.6 — ZFD-conditioned unit-rank reading with distance-aware amplitude law; supersedes v0.5].** The radius of convergence of the Fisher expansion I_K(1+ε) = ∂²_β log ζ_K about β=1 equals the distance from s = 1 to the nearest zero of (s−1)ζ_K(s) = the nearest zero of ζ_K (the (s−1) factor cancels the pole, adds no zero). The trivial-zero structure is fixed by signature (Lemma E.1): ord_{s=0} ζ_K = r₁+r₂−1 (the Dirichlet unit rank), ord_{s=−(2j−1)} = r₂, ord_{s=−2j} = r₁+r₂ (j ≥ 1); the nearest trivial zero lies at R_triv = 1 if rank ≥ 1 (zero at s=0), = 2 if rank 0 with r₂ ≥ 1 (imaginary quadratic, zero at s=−1), = 3 if rank 0 with r₂ = 0 (Q, zero at s=−2). **Unconditional spine:** rank ≥ 1 ⟹ radius ≤ 1 (the s=0 zero binds at distance 1); equivalently radius ≥ 2 ⟹ rank 0. Direction radius = 1 ⟹ rank ≥ 1 holds under the mild regularity that no rank-0 field carries a nontrivial zero at distance exactly 1 (standard zero-free regions near Re s = 1). **ZFD(K) condition for equality:** radius = R_triv iff ζ_K has no nontrivial zero in the punctured disk 0 < |s−1| < R_triv (hypothesis ZFD(K)); in particular rank ≥ 1 ⟹ radius = 1 iff ZFD(K), which excludes an exceptional (Siegel) real zero in (0,1) and any low-lying complex zero inside the unit disk. ZFD(K) is not implied by GRH: a zero at ½+it lies inside the unit disk whenever |t| < √3/2 ≈ 0.866. **Distance-aware amplitude law (Lemma E.1):** under ZFD(K), with G(ε) = log[(s−1)ζ_K(1+ε)], log-series coefficients b_k, R = radius, and m = order of the binding zero, |b_k|·k·R^k → m. For rank ≥ 1 (R = 1) this reduces to |b_k|·k → m = r₁+r₂−1 — the expansion reads the exact unit rank; for rank 0 it reads order 1 at the binding trivial zero (Q at R=3, imaginary quadratic at R=2). **Status.** [V] for K = Q and Q(√−3) (rank 0), Q(√5) and Q(√2) (rank 1), and the cyclic cubic of conductor 7 / signature (3,0) (rank 2): amplitude law verified |b_k|·k·R^k → m to 6–7 significant figures at orders 1 and 2 and radii 1/2/3 (module_e_radius_finding.py, cubic probe, amplitude-law check). For the three rank-≥1 fields, ZFD verified by direct argument-principle zero count — (1/2πi)∮_{|s−1|=r} ζ_K′/ζ_K ds returns −1 (zero interior zeros) inside |s−1| < 0.90 to 1e−7 for all three, refined to 1e−12 at radius 0.98 on Q(√5) (the r=0.98 discretization wobble self-identifies, scaling with m, and vanishes under node refinement); the b_k tail independently pins radius = 1 to seven figures, closing the outer annulus. Two independent methods bracket ZFD for the tested fields. [E → prove], conditional: the general rank ≥ 1 ⟹ radius = 1 and the rank-reading hold under ZFD(K) per field; not unconditionally provable (would need a no-Siegel-zero theorem plus a uniform low-lying-zero bound). Supporting proof: Lemma E.1 (proof document T1_lemma_E1_proof_v0_2.md).
